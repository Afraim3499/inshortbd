# Content Templates for Inshort

## Standard News Article Template

```
[HEADLINE - 50-70 characters, Title Case]

[EXCERPT - 150-300 characters, standalone summary]

[FEATURED IMAGE - 16:9 aspect ratio, relevant to story]

[LEAD PARAGRAPH - Answers: Who, What, When, Where, Why, How]

[PARAGRAPH 2 - Additional context or background]

[PARAGRAPH 3 - Direct quote or data point]

[PARAGRAPH 4 - Development or reaction]

[PARAGRAPH 5 - Implications or next steps]

[CLOSING PARAGRAPH - Summary or impact]
```

### Example Structure

```markdown
# Senate Passes $500B Infrastructure Bill

The U.S. Senate voted 68-32 to approve a $500 billion infrastructure package, marking the largest public works investment in decades.

[Image: Senate chamber during vote]

The U.S. Senate passed a sweeping $500 billion infrastructure bill Wednesday, Dec. 7, in a bipartisan 68-32 vote that could reshape American transportation and energy systems.

The legislation allocates $200 billion for highway and bridge repairs, $150 billion for public transit, and $150 billion for renewable energy infrastructure. It marks the largest federal infrastructure investment since the 1950s.

"This is a historic moment for American infrastructure," said Sen. Jane Smith (D-Calif.), who co-authored the bill. "We're finally investing in the foundation of our economy."

The bill now moves to the House of Representatives, where it faces an uncertain future. House Speaker John Doe (R-Texas) said Tuesday he would bring it to a vote "in due time."

If signed into law, the package would create an estimated 2 million jobs over the next decade and modernize critical transportation networks across all 50 states.
```

## Breaking News Template

```
[BREAKING BANNER TEXT - Single sentence, urgent but accurate]

[HEADLINE - Present tense, immediate impact]

[EXCERPT - Most critical information only]

[FEATURED IMAGE - If available immediately]

[PARAGRAPH 1 - What just happened, confirmed facts only]

[PARAGRAPH 2 - Who is involved or affected]

[PARAGRAPH 3 - When and where it occurred]

[PARAGRAPH 4 - Initial reaction or statements]

[UPDATES SECTION - If story is developing]
```

### Breaking News Checklist

- [ ] Use breaking news banner only for time-sensitive, high-impact events
- [ ] Verify all facts before publishing
- [ ] Use present tense in headline
- [ ] Focus on confirmed information only
- [ ] Update banner text as story develops
- [ ] Remove banner when story is no longer breaking

## Feature Article Template

```
[HEADLINE - Compelling but accurate, may be more creative]

[EXCERPT - Teases the story's angle or human element]

[FEATURED IMAGE - High-quality, visually compelling]

[HOOK - Engaging opening that draws readers in]

[SETUP - Context and background]

[NARRATIVE BODY - Tell the story with facts woven throughout]

[QUOTES - Direct quotes from sources]

[ANALYSIS - Deeper insight or implications]

[RESOLUTION - Clear ending or call to action]
```

### Feature Writing Tips

- Start with a scene, person, or moment
- Use descriptive language sparingly but effectively
- Include multiple perspectives
- Build narrative tension
- End with impact or reflection

## Analysis/Opinion Template

**Note**: Opinion pieces must be clearly labeled. Use category "Analysis" and include author's credentials.

```
[HEADLINE - Clear about the analysis or argument]

[EXCERPT - Summarizes the key point or conclusion]

[FEATURED IMAGE - Relevant to the topic]

[THESIS - State your main argument upfront]

[EVIDENCE - Present facts and data supporting the argument]

[COUNTERARGUMENTS - Address opposing viewpoints]

[ANALYSIS - Deep dive into implications]

[CONCLUSION - Restate thesis with final thoughts]
```

## Category-Specific Guidelines

### Politics
- Always include party affiliations
- Verify voting records and statements
- Include campaign finance data when relevant
- Link to official sources (Congress.gov, WhiteHouse.gov)

### Tech
- Explain technical concepts clearly
- Include company statements from official sources
- Verify product claims independently
- Link to original announcements or documentation

### Culture
- Include release dates for entertainment
- Verify artist/creator information
- Include relevant cultural context
- Respect diverse perspectives

### Business
- Verify financial data from SEC filings or official reports
- Include market context
- Quote company representatives
- Link to original financial documents when possible

### World
- Convert time zones to local context
- Explain cultural or political context
- Verify information through multiple international sources
- Include official government statements

## SEO Optimization

### Headlines
- Include primary keyword naturally
- Keep under 60 characters for full display
- Use numbers when relevant ("5 Ways...", "2024 Review")

### Excerpts
- Include primary keyword in first 150 characters
- Write to stand alone (may appear in search results)
- Create curiosity without clickbait

### Content
- Use headers (H2, H3) to structure content
- Include relevant keywords naturally
- Link to related articles
- Use descriptive alt text for images

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Headlines**: Too vague, clickbait-y, or inaccurate
2. **Excerpts**: Copying the first paragraph verbatim
3. **Quotes**: Not attributing or misquoting
4. **Numbers**: Incorrect figures or outdated statistics
5. **Dates**: Wrong dates or time zone confusion
6. **Names**: Misspelled names or incorrect titles
7. **Links**: Broken links or linking to unreliable sources
8. **Images**: Wrong images or missing alt text
