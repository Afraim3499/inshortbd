# Inshort Editorial Style Guide

## Mission Statement

"Inshort" is a "Terminal for Truth" - a high-performance, information-dense news platform for modern digital natives who demand fast, unbiased updates.

---

## Voice & Tone

### Voice
- **Authoritative but Accessible**: Speak as an expert, but never condescend
- **Direct and Clear**: No fluff, no filler. Every word serves a purpose
- **Impartial**: Present facts without bias. Let readers form their own conclusions
- **Urgent but Not Sensational**: Breaking news requires immediacy, not hyperbole

### Tone
- **Professional**: Serious journalism, not clickbait
- **Confident**: We're telling the truth, not asking for permission
- **Modern**: Speak the language of digital natives
- **Respectful**: Even when covering difficult topics

---

## Writing Standards

### Headlines
- **Length**: 50-70 characters for optimal display
- **Style**: Use active voice. Present tense for breaking news
- **Format**: Title Case (Capitalize All Major Words)
- **No Clickbait**: Headlines must accurately reflect article content
- **Specificity**: Use concrete facts, not vague statements

**Good Examples:**
- "Senate Approves $500B Infrastructure Package"
- "Tech Giants Face EU Antitrust Investigation"
- "Local Election Results: Mayor Wins by 12-Point Margin"

**Bad Examples:**
- "You Won't Believe What Happened Next" ❌
- "Breaking: Important News" ❌
- "This One Trick Changed Everything" ❌

### Body Text

#### Paragraphs
- **Length**: 2-4 sentences per paragraph
- **Structure**: One idea per paragraph
- **Spacing**: Use whitespace liberally for readability

#### Sentences
- **Length**: Average 15-20 words. Vary length for rhythm
- **Structure**: Subject-verb-object. Keep it simple
- **Active Voice**: "The committee voted" not "A vote was taken"

#### Word Choice
- **Precision**: Use specific, concrete words
- **Simplicity**: Choose simple words over complex ones
- **Avoid Jargon**: Explain technical terms when necessary
- **No Clichés**: Find fresh ways to express ideas

## Fact-Checking Standards

### Before Publishing

1. **Primary Sources**: Always link to original sources
2. **Verification**: Confirm facts with at least 2 independent sources
3. **Attribution**: Credit all quotes and data
4. **Dates & Numbers**: Double-check all dates, statistics, and figures
5. **Names & Titles**: Verify spelling of all names and official titles

### Sources Hierarchy
1. **Primary**: Official documents, press releases, direct quotes
2. **Secondary**: Reputable news outlets, academic sources
3. **Tertiary**: Opinion pieces (clearly labeled)

## Article Structure

### Standard News Article

1. **Headline**: Clear, specific, factual
2. **Excerpt** (150-300 chars): Summary that stands alone
3. **Lead Paragraph**: Who, what, when, where, why, how
4. **Body**: Develop the story, add context, include quotes
5. **Conclusion**: Impact, next steps, or summary

### Feature Article

1. **Headline**: Compelling but accurate
2. **Excerpt**: Tease the story's angle
3. **Hook**: Strong opening that draws readers in
4. **Narrative**: Storytelling with facts woven throughout
5. **Resolution**: Clear ending or call to action

## Style Rules

### Capitalization
- **Titles**: Capitalize when used before a name (President Smith)
- **Organizations**: Full official names capitalized
- **Headlines**: Title Case
- **Categories**: Always capitalized

### Numbers
- **Spell out**: One through nine
- **Numerals**: 10 and above
- **Exceptions**: Percentages (5%), dates (Dec. 7), times (3 p.m.)

### Punctuation
- **Oxford Comma**: Use consistently
- **Em Dashes**: Use for emphasis or asides—sparingly
- **Ellipses**: Only for actual omissions in quotes...

### Dates & Times
- Format: "Dec. 7, 2024" or "December 7, 2024"
- Times: "3 p.m." or "15:00" (24-hour for international)
- Relative: "Yesterday," "last week" (only in breaking news)

## Image Guidelines

### Featured Images
- **Aspect Ratio**: 16:9 or 2:1
- **Resolution**: Minimum 1200px width
- **File Format**: JPEG or WebP
- **Alt Text**: Descriptive, specific, under 125 characters

### Image Selection
- **Relevance**: Must directly relate to article content
- **Quality**: High resolution, well-composed, professional
- **Permissions**: Only use images with proper licensing
- **Sensitivity**: Consider context and impact

---

## Breaking News Protocol

### When to Use Breaking News Banner

1. **Immediate Impact**: Events affecting many people
2. **Time-Sensitive**: News that becomes less relevant quickly
3. **Major Significance**: Historic or precedent-setting events
4. **Public Safety**: Warnings, alerts, emergency information

### Breaking News Standards

- **Verify First**: Confirm facts before breaking banner
- **Update Frequently**: Keep breaking news current
- **Clear Language**: Simple, direct statements
- **Source Attribution**: Include source if possible

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing, ensure:

- [ ] Headline is clear, accurate, and compelling
- [ ] Excerpt summarizes the article effectively
- [ ] Lead paragraph answers the 5 W's
- [ ] All facts are verified with sources
- [ ] Quotes are accurate and attributed
- [ ] Images are relevant and properly licensed
- [ ] No spelling or grammatical errors
- [ ] Links work and point to reliable sources
- [ ] Category is appropriate
- [ ] Slug is URL-friendly and unique

---

## Prohibited Content

- Unverified claims presented as fact
- Plagiarism or unattributed content
- Hate speech or discriminatory language
- Personal attacks or libel
- Copyrighted material without permission
- Sponsored content not clearly labeled
- Misleading headlines or clickbait
